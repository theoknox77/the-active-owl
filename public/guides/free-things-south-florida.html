Free Things to Do in South Florida

Updated February 2026 · By Tim Palladino

South Florida has a reputation for being expensive, and sure, you can drop $300 on a dinner in Boca if you want. But most of the best stuff here costs nothing. Between the beaches, parks, weekly markets, outdoor concerts, and free community events, you can fill an entire week without spending a dollar. Here's the list.

Free Beaches & Outdoor Spaces

Delray Municipal Beach

Atlantic Ave ends right at the beach, and it's free to walk in. Some of the best people-watching in South Florida, especially on Sunday mornings when beach volleyball gets going. No parking meter can stop you if you walk in from the Ave.

Delray Beach · Open all day

Spanish River Park (Boca Raton)

Huge park with ocean access, a tunnel under A1A to the beach, and decent picnic areas. Parking costs on weekends, but walking or biking in is free.

Boca Raton · Daily 8 AM to sunset

Okeeheelee Park (West Palm Beach)

800 acres of trails, lakes, and open space. You can walk, run, fish, or just sit. One of the most underrated free spots in the county.

West Palm Beach · Daily sunrise to sunset

Free Fitness & Yoga

Sunday Yoga in the Park

Veterans Park in Delray Beach hosts free yoga sessions on Sunday mornings. Bring your own mat, show up at 9 AM, and you're in. It fills up fast, so get there early.

Veterans Park, Delray Beach · Sundays 9 AM

Sunset Yoga at Mizner Park (Boca Raton)

Outdoor yoga at the amphitheater a few times a month. Check the Mizner Park events calendar for dates. Free, open to all levels, and the setting is hard to beat.

Mizner Park, Boca Raton · Check schedule

Beach Volleyball (Open Courts)

Delray Municipal Beach has public courts that are first-come, first-served most of the time. Sunday mornings are the most active if you want to join a pickup game.

Delray Municipal Beach · Daily

Free Markets & Fairs

Old School Square Farmers Market

Free to walk through, and worth a lap. Local produce, crafts, food trucks, and live music on weekends. It's a Sunday ritual for a lot of Delray locals.

Old School Square, Delray Beach · Sundays 9 AM – 2 PM

Mizner Park Outdoor Events

The amphitheater at Mizner hosts free concerts and events throughout the year. Check their calendar and show up. Lawn seating is free; chairs optional.

Mizner Park, Boca Raton · Seasonal

Green Market at Meyer Amphitheater

West Palm's Saturday market runs October through April. Free to enter, lots of local vendors, and it's right on the waterfront. One of the better markets in the county.

Meyer Amphitheater, West Palm Beach · Saturdays 9 AM – 1 PM (Oct–Apr)

Free Nightlife & Events

Clematis by Night (West Palm Beach)

Free outdoor concert series on Clematis Street, every Thursday evening. Local bands, good weather (usually), and no cover charge. One of those things that West Palm does right.

Clematis Street, West Palm Beach · Thursdays 6–9 PM

NFL Sundays at Boston's on the Beach

No cover, right on A1A in Delray, outdoor bar vibe. Watch the game with a drink, or just hang on the deck without ordering anything. Nobody's checking.

Delray Beach · Sundays during NFL season

Lake Worth Beach Sunset

Free parking if you time it right, and the Lake Worth Beach Casino building has a public promenade with one of the best views in the county at sunset. No tickets, no reservations.

Lake Worth Beach · Nightly at sunset

By City

  • Delray Beach: Beach yoga, volleyball, farmers market, Atlantic Ave stroll, Pineapple Grove galleries
  • Boca Raton: Spanish River Park, Mizner events, Red Reef Park snorkeling (bring your own gear)
  • West Palm Beach: Clematis by Night, Green Market, Okeeheelee Park, Kravis Center free plaza concerts
  • Lake Worth Beach: Beach promenade, Lake Avenue street art walk, Saturday night street festivals
  • Boynton Beach: Oceanfront Park, Intracoastal kayak launch points, Schoolhouse Children's Museum (free community days)
